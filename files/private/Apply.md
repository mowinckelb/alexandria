## The Pitch (Core)

**One-liner:** Alexandria is the sovereign cognitive transformation layer for AI — who you are, developing across every model, owned by you.

**The setup:** Every AI platform is building memory. All of it is a retention moat — it keeps you locked in. Three years of context with Claude is a chain. Your identity is fragmented across every AI you use, and none of them talk to each other.

**The product:** Alexandria gives every AI a persistent understanding of who you are — your values, taste, communication style, thinking patterns. Not facts and preferences. Cognitive development. Data stays on your Google Drive, encrypted. Works across any AI via MCP. No own models.

**The market:** MCP has won — 97M monthly downloads, backed by Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft under the Linux Foundation. 543+ clients, 10K+ servers. The protocol layer is settled. The identity layer is wide open.

**The whitespace:** The market has two categories: developer memory infrastructure (Mem0, $24M) and platform memory features (retention moats). Nobody is building the sovereign, model-agnostic cognitive transformation primitive. The closest competitor (ownself.ai) is blockchain-based, pre-launch, no visible traction. We're live.

**Why now:** Platform memory is getting better — which makes the problem worse. Better personalization means more painful lock-in. The more your AI knows you, the harder it is to leave. Alexandria is the insurance policy that makes switching costless and every AI better.

**Why this founder:** The product is the founder's lived experience. Benjamin built his own Constitution — values, worldview, taste, mental models, shadows — and uses it across every AI interaction. The product methodology comes from hundreds of hours of actual cognitive extraction. The founder is the first Author. The process of building the company IS R&D for the product.

**The ask:** The company runs on $101/month. It does not need capital to survive. Capital accelerates the de-risk ladder — seeding experiment, brand building, community, founder buffer. Three rungs: ~$50K answers existential questions, ~$200K-500K scales what works, ~$1M+ is Library for Labs. The investor chooses where to come in. No engineering hires — solo founder + AI agents.

**Phase 1 — Now (March-April):** Apply to Menlo Anthology, YC, Solo Founders, SPC. These provide capital + network + credibility. Angel conversations in parallel — don't wait for accelerator outcomes.

**Phase 2 — SF Landing (April-May):** Events, community (Cerebral Valley), warm intros to Tier 1 investors. Use accelerator acceptance (if any) as credibility signal. Seed the founding lineage — the experiment starts.

**Phase 3 — Summer (June-August):** Accelerator batch (if accepted). First real traction data from the seeding experiment. This data determines the story for the next raise.

**Phase 4 — Fall (September-October):** Seed round with real numbers. The de-risk ladder has been climbed. Story shifts from "conviction bet" to "here's what happened."

Key insight: the amount and terms are a conversation, not a declaration. Let the relationship and the data determine the right structure. Don't box in before the market tells you what it wants to pay.
