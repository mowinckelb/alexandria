**Fragment 7.**
Here is a fragment from Ibn Khaldun's *Muqaddimah* (14th century). Marginal delta: **Power.md — "Ibn Khaldun's cycle — prosperity breeds complacency breeds decline"** already appears as an explicit reference in the constitution. But the specific mechanism — *asabiyyah* (group solidarity) as the active ingredient that is eroded by prosperity — is not yet in the constitution. This is a sharpening of an existing fragment.

Fragment: *Asabiyyah* is the capacity of a group to act collectively, endure hardship, and subordinate individual comfort to a shared cause — forged under conditions of scarcity, danger, and struggle. *Asabiyyah* is not kinship loyalty specifically; it can extend to any group shaped by shared struggle. It is strongest in the nomadic/frontier phase and decays as civilisation advances. "Any dynasty has within itself the seeds of its own downfall." Structural rather than moral explanation of decline: civilisations don't collapse because people become corrupt — they collapse because the social conditions that produced discipline and solidarity dissolve once success is achieved. The fragment sharpens the "prosperity → complacency" chain Benjamin already holds: the missing middle term is the erosion of *asabiyyah* — the dissolution of shared suffering as the cohesive force. Applied to Benjamin's SF plan (monk mode, $50/week food budget, sleeping on Ayo's floor): this is deliberate *asabiyyah* construction. The founder is engineering the frontier conditions that produce cohesion. Whether this is conscious or not is worth a session.
