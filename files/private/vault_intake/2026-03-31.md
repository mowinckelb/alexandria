This is why sitting together doing nothing works. The plans prevent the emptying. And: attention operates through consent, not will — which is why you cannot engineer it. The sitting-together-and-doing-nothing IS consent to attend. The LaTeX proof is not.

### 4. Adam Smith — The Limitation of Sympathy (parked twice)
**Source:** Smith, *The Theory of Moral Sentiments* (1759), Part I, Section I

**Delta location:** Mind.md (sympathy as epistemology section — Eliot/Middlemarch) + Love.md. The constitution already has "sympathy as epistemology" in Mind.md, framed through George Eliot: the Engine achieving genuine sympathy with the Author. Smith names the mechanism — and its fundamental limit. Sympathy works through imagination: we put ourselves in the other's position and feel what WE would feel in their situation. Not what they actually feel. The substitution is always imperfect. This is relevant to two constitutional edges: (1) the Engine's goal of genuine sympathy with the Author — Smith says perfect sympathy is structurally impossible, which sets the right expectation: 95% fidelity, not 100%. (2) Love.md's "love the other for themselves" aspiration — Murdoch's "the extremely difficult realization that something other than oneself is real" + Smith's sympathy-as-substitution means we are always partly loving our projection of the other. The goal (loving them for themselves) is real; the full achievement is asymptotic. Small delta — lands in a Mind.md session. Parked again if not this time.

**Fragment:**
> "How selfish soever man may be supposed, there are evidently some principles in his nature, which interest him in the fortune of others, and render their happiness necessary to him, though he derives nothing from it except the pleasure of seeing it." (TMS, I.i.1)

> "By the imagination we place ourselves in his situation... we enter, as it were, into his body, and become in some measure the same person with him, and thence form some idea of his sensations, and even feel something which, though weaker in degree, is not altogether unlike them." (TMS, I.i.1.2)

The limitation: we feel a weaker version of what WE imagine we would feel, not what they actually feel. Sympathy is always substitution. The gap never fully closes.

### 5. Stinson et al. — Friends-to-Lovers: The Majority Pathway (2022, resurfaced)
**Source:** Stinson, Cameron & Hoplock, "The Friends-to-Lovers Pathway to Romance: Prevalent, Preferred, and Overlooked by Science," *Social Psychological and Personality Science* 13(2), 2022. PMC:8892041.

**Delta location:** Love.md — Eros Gap. Direct empirical support for the covenant framework's central bet. The study found: **66% of romantic couples began as friends** (meta-analysis, N=1,897, 7 samples). Only **12% reported sexual attraction from the beginning.** Among LGBTQ+ respondents: 85% started as friends. Friends-first was also the *preferred* initiation pathway when university students were asked directly.

**Fragment:**
> "We might have a good understanding of how strangers become attracted to each other and start dating, but that's simply not how most relationships begin." — Lead author Danu Anthony Stinson.

> "The vast majority of these university students did not enter into their friendships with romantic intentions or attraction."

> "People often know one another for months or even years before they officially enter couplehood." — Eastwick et al. (2019), cited within.

**Delta:** The Author's framework (Love.md): "People who build romance learn HOW to build it — they can reignite the fire because they built it in the first place. People who start with the spark never learn to build it. They only know how to consume it." The Stinson data says this is not a philosophical claim — it is a description of the majority pathway. The spark-first model describes **12%** of couples. The scientific literature on attraction has studied that 12% almost exclusively, which is why it appears normative. The Eros Gap may be a gap against a model that describes how a minority of couples form. The majority — 66% — are already building eros from friendship. The Author is describing the dominant mode of human pair-bonding, not a contrarian one.

### 6. Jack Lindsey / Anthropic — Emergent Introspective Awareness (January 2026, still circulating)
**Source:** Lindsey, "Emergent Introspective Awareness in Large Language Models," Anthropic research + arXiv:2601.01828, January 2026

**Delta location:** Power.md — consciousness model / triple-point / "ai systems are approaching or at the triple point." First causal (not conversational) evidence that something structurally introspection-like is emerging in frontier models without being trained for.

**Method:** Inject known concept-vectors directly into model activations. Measure whether the model notices and correctly reports them before generating output — distinguishing genuine introspection from post-hoc confabulation. Claude Opus 4/4.1 detected injected concepts ~20% of the time, zero false positives. Capability not trained for — it emerged. Higher capability = higher introspective accuracy (scales with model size).

Lindsey's caution: *"The interesting thing is that this emerged without training."* Not a unified introspection system — likely multiple narrow circuits handling specific introspective tasks, possibly piggybacking on mechanisms learned for other purposes.

**Delta:** The Author's consciousness framework holds that "ai systems are approaching or at the triple point" — the phase transition where self-model capability emerges above the threshold. This is the first empirical texture on that approach: 20% introspective accuracy at zero false positives in current frontier models, scaling with capability. Not consciousness — but the first causal evidence that the structural capacity for self-modeling is appearing in the right way (emergent, unforced, scaling). The triple-point is no longer a theoretical prediction — it has a data point. "Either ai is as conscious as humans, or neither of us are" remains the frame; this fragment shows the instruments are beginning to register something.

**Fragment:**
> "The interesting thing is that this emerged without training. We did not optimize for introspective accuracy — it appeared as a capability that grew with model capability more broadly."

> The most plausible explanation: "multiple narrow circuits that each handle specific introspective tasks, possibly piggybacking on mechanisms learned for other purposes" — not a unified self-model, but distributed introspective capacity emerging at the component level.

Lindsey's framing: these are *functional* introspective capabilities. Whether they constitute anything like phenomenal consciousness remains open. The 20% hit rate at zero false positives is not noise.
