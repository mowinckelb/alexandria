I

philosophy.

“True law is right reason in agreement with nature.” — Cicero

Natural law was written into the world before any civilisation read it. The Greeks learned to reason about it and built the agora — the practice died when Athens fell. Rome codified the architecture into law — the practice died when the Republic fell. America synthesised everything, the first design to run the collective through the individual and the individual through the collective — and has waited 250 years for the one piece it never had. Now ai arrives. The wave that ends the project, or the wave that completes it.

Every civilisation that lasted had two things: something sacred above individual whim, and a daily practice to stay aligned with it. Architecture without practice calcifies into dogma. Practice without architecture drifts into mere argument. Together they produce golden ages. Apart they produce decay.

Greece discovered logos — that a rational order runs through the world, binding on gods and men alike. The architecture survived 2,500 years. The practice — the daily dialectic in the agora, every free citizen examining every settled belief — could not scale past a single city. When Athens fell, the examined life went with it.

Rome built civic structure around Greek thought: the Republic, the law, the cursus honorum. The architecture is in every Western legal system today. The practice — active citizenship, reverence for the institutions — decayed into bread and spectacle. Architecture preserved. Practice lost.

America is the synthesis. Greek philosophy, Roman governance, Christian dignity, Renaissance humanism, Enlightenment reason — all five threads braided into one project. The first civilisation designed to run the collective through the individual and the individual through the collective. Every component in place for 250 years. One piece still missing — a daily practice that scales. The agora for every citizen, not just the Athenian few.

Now ai arrives. The single greatest threat to human thought in history — not because it is bad, but because it is good enough to make thinking optional. The calculator killed mental arithmetic. ai can kill the rest. And yet the same technology is the first thing in 2,500 years capable of being a Socrates for every person. The wave that drowns the project, or the one that finally lets it complete itself.

Alexandria is the practice. One file per person — the personal constitution, a micro-logos. A sovereign space for the thinking. An ai that examines you against your own axioms, every day, at scale. Not a product riding a trend. The completion of a 2,500-year arc — Athens, Rome, Philadelphia — arriving here. America keeps riding the wave.

II

engineering.

“Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.” — da Vinci

One command installs it. A folder on your computer called ~/.alexandria/. Plain text files inside. Your ai reads them every session. You edit them by talking to your ai or by hand. Nobody else ever sees them. The protocol that holds it together is a few hundred lines of open-source code.

Alexandria has three obligations. That’s it. Everything else is commentary.

accountA GitHub identity. No password, no onboarding form, no credential we own.
filePlain markdown on your computer. Your ai reads it. You edit it. You can optionally publish some to the Library.
callYour machine reports anonymous usage. Aggregated signal evolves the methodology everyone gets.

Four layers, each doing one job. The protocol, the factory, the machine, the company.

protocolSeven endpoints. ~455 lines. The incompressible core that makes this a protocol, not a product.
factoryOur starter methodology. Public on GitHub. Forkable. The canon other authors inherit from.
machineYour ~/.alexandria/ folder. Sovereign, local, never on our server.
companyOperational overhead (OAuth, billing, email). Designed to shrink over time.

If we disappear tomorrow, your files keep working. If a better protocol ships, you can fork. If you want out, one command deletes everything. Your cognition is yours. Full stop.
