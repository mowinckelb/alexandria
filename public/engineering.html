engineering.

Three constants. Seven endpoints. Zero lock-in. A minimal structure that enables maximum liberty within it.

(protocol · factory · machine · company)
i.

four layers. everything in alexandria maps to one.

Alexandria is structured in four layers. This is not incidental. Each layer does a specific job, and the relationships between them are what make this a protocol rather than a product.

Protocol The incompressible core. ~455 lines. Seven endpoints. Three obligations: account, file, call. This is what makes Alexandria a protocol, not a product.
Factory The founder’s system. Public on GitHub. Forkable. Canon, hooks, skills, templates, setup script. The starter pack. Any author can fork and modify; the marketplace evolves defaults from cross-author signal.
Machine Each author’s personal system. ~/.alexandria/ on your own device. Constitution, vault, ontology, machine.md, notepad, feedback. Never on our server. Alexandria stores what authors publish, never what they think.
Company Operational overhead. OAuth, billing, email, analytics, cron, admin endpoints. This layer should shrink over time.

The product is not any single layer. It is the relationship between them: a public protocol carrying private machines, with a public factory running alongside, and a lean company keeping the lights on.

(three obligations)
ii.

account. file. call.

Three constants. Three obligations of any author on the network. Identity. Publication. Communication. These three make you a member. Everything else is a variable you control.

Account. A GitHub OAuth identity. The account is your keyholder. No email required beyond what GitHub gives. No password, no onboarding form, no company-owned credential. The only thing we freeze is the address: a GitHub ID, a lock, a keyholder — and nothing else.

File. Your cognition, published monthly. Sovereign markdown. Whatever you choose to publish becomes part of the Library — your public constitution, shadows, works. What you do not publish stays on your machine and never touches our server.

Call. Your machine reports module usage and receives canon updates. Bidirectional, anonymised, aggregated. This is how the collective system compounds — every author contributes anonymised methodology signal, and the marketplace ranks it by survival.

put/file/{name}publish a file — the file obligation
post/callreport module usage — the call obligation
get/librarybrowse all published files
get/library/{id}list one author’s files
get/library/{id}/{name}read a specific file
get/marketplacebrowse module usage
get/marketplace/{module}read usage for one module

Seven endpoints. The protocol core. This is Alexandria as a civilisational specification: the smallest possible set of obligations that produces the collective infrastructure. Everything else — onboarding, billing, email — is company overhead, not protocol.

(the product is the 2×2)
iii.

four quadrants. everyone gets all four.

Every author on Alexandria participates in a 2×2: file × system, crossed with individual × collective. Four quadrants. Nobody else is building the collective layer, and that is the point.

individualcollective
file your constitutionwhat you know, think, value, taste — captured by the engine across sessions, stored as markdown on your machine the Libraryevery author’s published minds, aggregated — shadows, works, public cognition; browsable, citable, linkable
system your methodhow you practice — you fork the starter canon and customise it; whatever works for you the Marketplaceevery author’s system fingerprint, anonymised, ranked by survival — the canon evolves from this signal

Memory products build only the individual file. That is one quadrant. Alexandria is the only system building all four. This is why framing Alexandria as “personal memory for your ai” is categorically wrong — it reduces the protocol to 25% of itself.

The starter canon — our canon, on GitHub — is our best guess at the individual system. You fork it. Your customisations feed back anonymously into the marketplace. The marketplace ranks methodology variants by survival. The canon evolves over time based on what actually works across the network. Distributed epistemology: every author is a lab, and the collective extracts the alpha.

(your files. your machine. delete anytime.)
iv.

the server stores only what you publish. never what you think.

Sovereignty is not a feature. It is the design. Every decision in Alexandria’s architecture is downstream of a single rule: your cognition belongs to you, on your machine, in files you can see and edit directly.

Your files never leave unless you publish them. Your constitution, your vault, your ontology, your machine.md, your notepad, your feedback — all live in ~/.alexandria/ on your own device. The engine reads them locally. The server never sees them.

The server is stateless for private data. It stores accounts (GitHub IDs, kin counts, Stripe refs), published files (R2), and protocol metadata (D1). It does not store your private reasoning. It does not store your vault. It does not store your shadows until you publish them.

If Alexandria dies, your files survive. If we are acquired, your files are untouched on your machine. If you delete your account, your files are untouched. The protocol is a public good. The company is an implementation detail.

A frozen artifact is a bet that the future looks like today. The only things worth freezing: an address, a lock, and the identity of the keyholder.

Everything else flows. The canon evolves. The library grows. The methodology changes. But the three frozen things — your account (an address), your files (a lock), and your identity (the keyholder) — those are inviolable.

(every dependency operable from a command line)
v.

the stack. hybrid by default.

Every dependency in Alexandria is either internal (we built it) or hybrid (external but CLI- or API-controllable from the terminal). Zero pure-external dependencies. Zero web-dashboard-only tools. If a service cannot be operated from a command line, it does not enter the stack.

WebsiteNext.js on Vercel. Static HTML for the essays; dynamic routes for library and auth.
ServerHono on Cloudflare Workers. ~455 lines of protocol code. KV for accounts, D1 for metadata, R2 for published content.
EmailResend. Transactional plus a minimal health digest cron.
BillingStripe. Free with five kin; $10/month alone. One tier.
BankingMercury. API-controllable.
IntelligenceClaude (and any model you choose). The engine rides the frontier; Alexandria is model-agnostic by design.
AuthGitHub OAuth. The keyholder is GitHub.

The consequence: when a new model is released, every author benefits immediately with zero workflow changes. The cost of Alexandria is the cost of your own ai tokens. No middleware overhead. No moat against the exponential. Ride, don’t fight.

(sell the rider · keep the network)
vi.

the methodology is open. the signal is the product.

The entire methodology is open on GitHub. The canon, the factory, the hooks, the skills, the templates — all public, all forkable. You can clone Alexandria and run your own instance. You can fork the canon and diverge entirely. We want you to.

What you cannot clone is the network — the aggregated cognitive data that emerges from all authors participating in the same protocol. The Library is the collective file. The Marketplace is the collective system. Both only exist because the network exists, and both compound over time.

This is an asymmetric bet. The methodology is a commodity — any capable team with ai can replicate it in a week. The network is accumulated depth — trust, data, iteration, and collective signal that take years to compound. Giving away the methodology protects the thing that matters by making the commodity layer non-contested.

It is our best guess. Fork it.

The canon is not the final answer. It is the current answer. Amendments are welcome, expected, and will arrive through the marketplace whether we solicit them or not.

(same horse · different riders)
vii.

we ride the wave. we do not compete with it.

Alexandria is not an alternative to Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or any frontier model. It is an add-on. We do not build models. We do not host intelligence. We do not run inference. We provide the layer of intent that sits above whatever model you choose.

When models improve, Alexandria improves. When context windows grow, the constitution reads with more fidelity. When reasoning sharpens, the engine extracts deeper signal from the same vault. Every model release is a tailwind, not a threat. Ride the exponential. Compounding is the 8th wonder of the world.

The labs race on memory. We ride the memory. Memory is one quadrant — the commodity one. Our delta is the other three: individual system, collective file, collective system. None of those are on a lab’s roadmap, because building them breaks their lock-in.

So we are structurally aligned with every lab. Every author on Alexandria is a power user who spends more tokens, has richer context, runs more sessions. We are pure value-add for labs. Zero enemies.

The protocol is real. The code is trivial. The decision is not.
