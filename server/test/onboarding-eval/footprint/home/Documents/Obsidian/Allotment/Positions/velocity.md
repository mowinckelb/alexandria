# velocity

Most of the cost of moving fast is paid by people who weren't in the room.

That's the whole position. Everything else is footnote.

"Move fast and break things" never says *whose* things. The people breaking them are never the people who get broken. Speed is cheap for whoever holds the wheel and expensive for everyone in the road. So when someone sells me velocity as a virtue, my first question is the [[power-not-capability|power question]]: fast *for whom*, at whose cost.

This is not timidity. I ship. The dispatch guardrail shipped in a week and it was right to. Slowness isn't the goal — **legibility** is the goal, and speed is the thing that most reliably destroys it. You move fast by not asking who's downstream. The asking is the slow part. The asking is the point.

Corollary for writing: most writing advice optimises volume, so most writing advice is wrong for me. Publish rarely, revise obsessively. A thing isn't done because I'm tired of it. See [[founder-myth]] on the same disease in a different costume — the myth that the individual sprinting alone is the unit of progress.

Where this bites me: the co-op. I want to move fast on it and moving fast means doing it alone means paying the cost myself which sounds noble and is actually just me being the one in the room deciding for people who aren't. Watch that.
