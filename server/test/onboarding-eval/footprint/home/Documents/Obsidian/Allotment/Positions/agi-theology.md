# agi as theology

The intelligence explosion is a theology with a compute budget.

The claim "scaling → AGI → discontinuity" presents as an engineering forecast. It isn't. It's a political claim wearing a forecast's clothes. Watch the move: if the explosion is *inevitable*, then you don't regulate it (can't stop the tide), you don't ask who benefits (there's no time), you just get on the train or get left behind. Inevitability is the most powerful political argument there is because it disguises a choice as a fact. See [[power-not-capability]] — same smuggle, top shelf.

So: skeptic. The scaling story is ideology dressed as a curve.

**And here's the part I refuse to sand smooth.**

I work in ML. I have *watched scaling work* on my own systems — thrown more data and more compute at a stubborn problem and watched it just... give, in a way that felt less like engineering and more like weather. I don't get to pretend I haven't seen that. And worse: a lot of the people who believe the explosion hardest look down on people like me, the cautious ones, the ecologists, the "she doesn't get it" crowd. And I cannot fully separate my skepticism from my status. Some of my "it won't happen" is honest analysis and some of it is cope — a defence against being the person who didn't see it coming, who got left in the road (see [[velocity]]).

I keep the skepticism and I keep the fear that the skepticism is cope. Both. I won't resolve it to feel clean.

That's not a placeholder. That's the position. A person who needs this resolved by Thursday is a person who'll take whichever answer lets them sleep, and I don't trust that person. Hold both. Argue from the held thing.
