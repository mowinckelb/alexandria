# degrowth (unresolved)

I believe in abundance for the many through better allocation, not more extraction.

I cannot stand the degrowth scene.

Both true, and I keep circling the gap between them without closing it.

The economics I mostly buy: the planet is a closed system, infinite-growth-on-a-finite-substrate is a mathematical error we've dressed up as ambition, and the honest project is *distribution* — most scarcity is a routing failure, not a supply failure. There is enough. There has been enough for a long time. The problem is who it's routed to. (Same shape as everything else I think — see [[power-not-capability]].)

But the *aesthetics*. The hairshirt. The purity contests about whether you're allowed to fly, the quiet satisfaction some of them take in less, the way "sufficiency" curdles into a moral hierarchy of who consumes least. It reads as austerity with better PR, and austerity has never once been paid by the people who prescribe it. I don't want a smaller life. I want a *fairly allocated abundant* one, and I don't have the word for that yet — "degrowth" is taken and it's wearing a hemp shirt.

So I'm stuck between an analysis I hold and an aesthetic I can't stomach, and I suspect the aesthetic allergy is partly aesthetic snobbery on my part, which doesn't make the allergy wrong but does mean I don't get to feel superior about it.

Leaving this open. Not everything gets a bow.
