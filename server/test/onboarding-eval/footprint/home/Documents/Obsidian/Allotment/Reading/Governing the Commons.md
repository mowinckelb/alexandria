# Governing the Commons — Ostrom

Not a summary. Reactions.

- The whole "tragedy of the commons" was Hardin assuming the commoners were strangers who couldn't talk to each other. They can talk. That one assumption is doing *all* the work in his tragedy. Remove it and the tragedy evaporates. Furious this took me until 34 to read.
- Her design principles read like a spec for the co-op. Clear boundaries, rules made by the people bound by them, cheap conflict resolution, monitoring by the users themselves. → this is [[legibility]], legible-downward, in institutional form.
- p.90ish — graduated sanctions. not zero tolerance, not the hammer. the community that survives punishes *proportionally* and keeps you in it. margin: "guilds not monocultures again"
- the thing the economists called impossible was just illegible to their model. the commons worked for centuries; the theory that said it couldn't was the thing that was broken. classic [[power-not-capability]] adjacent — they measured the wrong axis.

carry into the talk? maybe. she's the antidote to inevitability — proof that "there is no alternative" is almost always a failure of imagination funded by whoever benefits from no alternative.
