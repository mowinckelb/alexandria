# "the scaling hypothesis" reading — critical AI pile

been reading the accelerationist canon *and* its critics side by side so i don't strawman anyone on Thursday. notes on the critical side.

- the move i keep catching: "emergence." a capability shows up at scale that wasn't there before → therefore *more scale → more emergence → intelligence explosion.* but emergence-so-far is not a law, it's a pattern, and dressing a pattern as a law is exactly the theology move. see [[agi-theology]].
- the papers arguing "emergent abilities are a mirage of the metric" — change the metric from all-or-nothing to continuous and the sharp jump smooths out. i find this *very* convincing and i distrust exactly how much i want it to be true. (see [[Compost/cope test]]. yeah.)
- the political tell is always the same: whenever the forecast concludes "therefore we can't stop / can't regulate / can't wait to ask who benefits," check whose interest that conclusion serves. the forecast that happens to also be the business plan deserves a second look. [[power-not-capability]].
- but — and i have to keep this honest — i have watched scale just *work* on Aurora's own models. i don't get to wave that away because it's inconvenient for my talk. hold both.

for the talk: don't argue AGI won't happen. argue that "it's inevitable" is a claim about politics smuggled in a claim about compute. that's the defensible line and it's the true one.
