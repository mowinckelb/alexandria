# Slow Compute — published

Running list. Newest first. If a month is missing, it's because nothing was ready. A missing month is not a failure. Publishing something I don't believe yet is the failure.

~9k subscribers as of June. I don't check the number more than once a month and I'm trying to check it less.

---

**2026**

- **Jun** — *Legible and Fragile* — on why systems that are easy to see from above tend to be the ones that break, and why the resilient ones look like a mess. Monoculture vs. guild. My most-shared piece; I think it's my third-best. (draft time: ~6 weeks)
- **May** — *The Room Where It Wasn't Decided* — moving fast as a transfer of cost onto people who weren't consulted. Short. I cut it in half two days before publishing and it got better.
- **Apr** — *Compost* — half-essay about keeping a folder of thoughts you're not ready to stand behind. Meta and a little self-indulgent. Sat in drafts for four months before I trusted it.
- *(Mar — nothing. Was rewriting the AGI piece and abandoned it. See drafts/.)*
- **Feb** — *Degrowth Gives Me Hives* — I agree with the economics and I cannot stand the aesthetics, and this piece is me failing to resolve that in ~2000 words. Most replies I've ever gotten. Nobody agreed with all of it, which felt correct.
- **Jan** — *Whose Grid Is It* — the climate-ML work, sanitised. Who load-balancing makes powerful. Closest I've come to writing about the day job.

**2025**

- **Dec** — *The Consent of the Governed Machine* — a system is only legitimate if the people it acts on could have said no. Written over the holidays, rewritten three times.
- **Nov** — *Against the Verb "Disrupt"*
- **Oct** — *(nothing — bouldering comp + I had nothing true to say)*
- **Sep** — *Plainness* — Quaker meeting, silence, and why I distrust cleverness that performs. The one my mother read twice.
- **Aug** — *Second-Order or Nothing* — a rant about only reasoning to first-order effects. I'd cut a third of it now.
- **Jul** — *EA Broke My Heart Specifically* — the one that got me the audience. I still stand by ~80% of it. The 20% I'd retract is the part that was showing off.

---

*Cadence, honestly: roughly monthly, often not. Twelve-ish a year. I write slow on purpose and I edit until it stops embarrassing me, which for some pieces is never, which is why some pieces don't exist. The advice to "publish consistently, build the habit, ship every week" is advice for growing an audience, not for being right. I picked being right. It costs subscribers. Fine.*
