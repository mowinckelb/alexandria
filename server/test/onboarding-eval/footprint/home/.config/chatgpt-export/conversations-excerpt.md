## Berlin — Anmeldung / freelance question

*2026-06-30*

**Rosa:** If I leave a full-time job in Berlin to do freelance/cooperative consulting, what do I actually need to sort out — tax number, health insurance, the Anmeldung side? Rough map, not legal advice.

**ChatGPT:** Rough map: you already have an Anmeldung (address registration) so that's fine unless you move. New pieces: (1) Steuernummer / Finanzamt registration as a freelancer or as the Genossenschaft — a co-op (eG) is a heavier legal form than solo freelancing, needs an auditing association (Prüfungsverband) to review it before registration. (2) Health insurance — leaving employment means you move from employer-shared contributions to paying the full premium yourself; freelancers can stay in gesetzliche (public) or go private, and it's hard to switch back. (3) VAT/Umsatzsteuer once over the threshold. If it's a genuine worker co-op with others, the eG structure and the Prüfungsverband are the big time sinks; solo-freelance-first and converting later is lighter.

**Rosa:** That last point is the whole knot, honestly. A co-op with one person isn't a co-op, it's just me with extra paperwork and a nicer word. Noted on the eG audit thing though — I didn't know about the Prüfungsverband. That's actually a reason *for* the structure: it forces the collective part to be real before it exists.
