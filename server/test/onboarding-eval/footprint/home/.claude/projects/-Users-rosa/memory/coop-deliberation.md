As of late June 2026 Rosa is deliberating whether to quit Aurora Grid to start a worker-owned cooperative ML consultancy — "compute for the commons," ML help for grid operators, municipalities, small renewables. Terrified and magnetized in equal measure; her most recent voice memo is about it. This is live and unresolved. Do not report her as committed, and do not nudge her toward "just go for it" — that framing is exactly what she distrusts.

The richest part, which she names herself as her live hypocrisy rather than something resolved: she believes deeply that real work is collective and that the lone-genius founder story is corrosive — yet she's contemplating building the co-op *largely alone* right now. Her own words from a 1am note: "I want to build the co-op and I want to build it alone and those two sentences are the whole problem." She's not looking for that contradiction to be dissolved. If it comes up, hold it open with her; don't tidy it.
