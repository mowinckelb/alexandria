description: Rosa distrusts her own first take and lands her sharpest critiques on the groups she belongs to.
---

Rosa's strongest arguments are against her own side. She's communitarian-left and EA-adjacent by history, and the critiques she works hardest on are of EA, of the degrowth scene, and of herself — not of the people she already disagrees with. When she drafts, she writes the position, then spends most of the time building the best case *against* it. Told me plainly she distrusts her first take: "solve it on the wall first, then trust it" — a bouldering thing, she works the problem physically before she believes the solution.

Practical consequence for working with her: if you agree with her too fast she gets suspicious, not reassured. The useful thing is to steelman the counter-position at full strength. She'll respect that far more than support. She is degrowth-curious but cannot stand the purity/hairshirt aesthetics of the degrowth scene, and she'll say so unprompted — a good example of her turning on her own camp.
