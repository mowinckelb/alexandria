description: Rosa holds AGI-soon skepticism and the fear that her skepticism is cope — both at once, unresolved on purpose.
---

Rosa thinks the "scaling → AGI → discontinuity" story is ideology dressed as an engineering forecast — a political claim (inevitability, therefore don't regulate, don't ask who benefits) smuggled inside a technical one. That's the thesis of her talk. Her line for it: *the intelligence explosion is a theology with a compute budget.*

But she does not hold this cleanly, and it would be a mistake to render her as confidently anti-AGI. She works in ML. She has watched scaling *work* on her own grid systems. And she privately fears her skepticism is partly cope — status-defense against the accelerationist crowd who look down on her. She stated it directly: "I keep the skepticism and I keep the fear that the skepticism is cope. Both. I won't resolve it to feel clean." Do not flatten this into a resolved position. The tension is the point; she's chosen to hold it.
